About the hackathon

The MS-CC Environmental Science Hackathon at WVSU invites students to tackle real-world sustainability and rural resilience challenges using data science, AI, and open-source tools — with a cybersecurity mindset.

Why this matters

Distributed networks of low-cost sensors are used where traditional monitoring is limited. Communities may rely on this data for health, environmental, and operational decisions.

Threats are subtle

Sensors can report faulty data, APIs can be misused, and decision systems can be misled by incomplete or manipulated signals. Your job is to detect and defend.

Realistic learning

You’ll work like a security professional protecting data pipelines under uncertainty—skills that transfer to public health, environmental monitoring, and IoT systems.

The challenge

Build a sensor trust model that helps identify unreliable or compromised air quality sensors.
